The Politics of Design

Colour and Culture

RED

comes from the Sanskrit word for blood. In Greek mythology it was the color for the planet Mars and the god of war. Red is the most important color in China and means good luck and happiness. Red promotes long life, and it is used in Chinese new year celebrations. Red is the traditional color for brides in India and Nepal. In Japan, a red kimono symbolizes happiness and good luck. In Japan's Shinto religion, red is the symbol of life. The Russian word for red has the same root in Old Russian as the word 'beautiful'. The oldest symbol of socialism is the reel flag, and red is still associated with socialist and communist parties. The red shirts, or the red movement, was the name of those who opposed the 2006 military coup in Thailand.

BLUE

The color blue is tested as the color that is best liked by all cultures, which is how it became the color for international business. The United Nations chose blue as a color in 1946, but the color has no special significance. Bluewashing is a term for companies that join the United Nations 'Global Impact Initiative' only for PR reasons. 'Blue collarworker'refers to the shirts wornby people working manual labor jobs. The Tuareg of North Africa wear blue turbans, dyed with indigo. Azure blue is thetraditional color of Italy, the national soccer team is called the Azzurri. Several languages, like Japanese, Thai, Korean, and Lakota Sioux, use the same word to describe blue and green.

GREEN

is the color of nature in many cultures. Early rituals centered on the hope of a good harvest with green vegetables. Green is the traditional color of Islam, the color of the prophet Muhammed, which is why many flags in the Islamic world use the color green. Since the 1980s, green has become the color of environmental parties and organizations. Green parties in Europe have programmes based on ecology and environment. The word greenwashing is used to describe the advertising of companies that use positive environmental practices to cover up environmentally unfriendly activities. A 'green room' is used in television and theater to quiet nervous performers.

PURPLE

was the most expensive dye in Roman times, and was worn by Roman magistrates, Byzantine emperors, and the rulers of the Holy Roman Empire. During the reign of Elizabeth I, only royalty was allowed to wear purple in England. Roman Catholic bishops still wear purple as a color of royalty. In Japan, the color is traditionally associated with theemperor and aristocracy. In Thailand, widows in mourning wearpurple. In Western politics the color purple is neutral, and used for a coalition between a left-wing and a right-wing party. The Women's Suffrage movement used purple, and the color was adopted in the 1970s by the women's liberation movement. Purple and pink are bot h associated with the LGBT community.
