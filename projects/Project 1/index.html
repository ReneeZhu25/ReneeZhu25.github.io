Chinese Oracle Bone Script




The Chinese Oracle Bone Script was the earliest form of the Chinese script.

꩜

It was first used over 3000 years ago during the late Shang Dynasty.

꩜

The scripts were pictographic forms that were carved on
ox bones and tortoise shells.

꩜

Bone oracles were used to communicate with ancestors who were
believed to have the power to bestow guidance on the living world.

꩜

The first Chinese oracle bone script found was in Xiaotun in the late 1800's.

꩜

Official excavations began at the site in 1928 and the bones were authenticated by Li Ji
(also known as Li Chi).

꩜

Chinese Oracle Bones scripts have evolved into the modern Chinese characters seen today.
