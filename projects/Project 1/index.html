Chinese Oracle Bone Script

The Chinese Oracle Bone Script was the earliest form of the Chinese script. It was first used over 3000 years ago during the late Shang Dynasty.

The scripts were rectilinear and PICTOGRAPHIC FORMS that were carved on OX SHOULDER BONES and TORTOISE SHELLS, also known as plastron.

The royal household used bone oracles to communicate with ancestors and deities and was believed to have the power to bestow fortune, disasters, and guidance on the living world.

The first Chinese oracle bone script found was in XIAOTUN by local farmers in the late 1800's.

Official excavations began at the site in 1928 and the bones were authenticated by LI JI (also known as LI CHI).

Chinese Oracle Bones scripts have evolved into the MODERN CHINESE SCRIPT seen today.
